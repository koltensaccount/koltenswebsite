Section 3.6, Problem 4

Use cofactor expansions to evaluate the determinant. Expand along the row or column that minimizes the amount of computation... [cite: 50]

\[ \begin{vmatrix} 5 & 11 & 8 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & 6 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & 0 & 17 \end{vmatrix} \]

Solution: [cite: 51]

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{det}(A) &= 0-0+0-3\begin{vmatrix} 5 & 11 & 8 \\ 3 & -2 & 6\\ 0 & 4 & 0 \end{vmatrix} \\ &= -3(0-4\begin{vmatrix} 5 & 8 \\ 3 & 6\end{vmatrix}+0) \\ &= -3(-4(5*6-3*8)) \\ &= \boxed{72} \end{aligned} \]

Section 3.6, Problem 16

Use the method of elimination to evaluate the determinant. [cite: 52]

\[ \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 4 & -2 \\ 5 & -4 & 1 \\ 4 & 2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} \]

Solution: [cite: 53, 54, 55]

The determinant of a triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries.

\[ |A| = 4 \cdot (1) \cdot (-7) \cdot (\tfrac{17}{7}) = 4 \cdot (-17) = \boxed{-68} \]
