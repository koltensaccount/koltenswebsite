Section 3.4, Problem 23

Find $B$ so that $AB=I=BA$... [cite: 17, 18]

\[ \mathbf{A}=\begin{bmatrix}2&1\\3&2\end{bmatrix},\quad \mathbf{B}=\begin{bmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{bmatrix} \]

Solution: [cite: 19, 20]

\[ AB= \begin{bmatrix} 2a+c & 2b+d\\ 3a+2c & 3b+2d \end{bmatrix} =I \;\Rightarrow\; \begin{cases} 2a+c=1,\\ 3a+2c=0,\\ 2b+d=0,\\ 3b+2d=1. \end{cases} \]

...Hence

\[ B=\begin{bmatrix}2&-1\\-3&2\end{bmatrix}. \]

Section 3.5, Problem 6

First apply the Inverses of $2 \times 2$ Matrices Theorem to find $\mathbf{A^{-1}}$. Then use $\mathbf{A^{-1}}$ to solve the system $\mathbf{Ax=b}$ [cite: 25]

\[\mathbf{A}=\begin{bmatrix}4&7\\3&6\end{bmatrix},\mathbf{b}=\begin{bmatrix}10\\5\end{bmatrix}\]

Solution: [cite: 26, 27]

First we verify that $\mathbf{A}$ is invertible... $det(\mathbf{A})=(4)(6)-(7)(3)=3\ne0$

\[ \mathbf{A^{-1}}=\frac{1}{3}\begin{bmatrix}6&-7\\-3&4\end{bmatrix} \] \[ \mathbf{x}=(A^{-1})b=\begin{bmatrix}2&-\frac{7}{3}\\-1&\frac{4}{3}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}10\\5\end{bmatrix}=\boxed{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{25}{3}\\-\frac{10}{3}\end{bmatrix}} \]
